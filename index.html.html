Module Overview

What is PRESTO Transition and why does it matter to you as frontline staff?

What is PRESTO Transition?
PRESTO Transition is the largest technology upgrade to PRESTO in recent years. Physical PRESTO cards are moving from stored value cards (data on the chip) to account-linked cards (data stored online). This means real-time balance updates, instant loads, and no more tap-to-confirm.

Card Conversion

How the conversion works, what customers will notice, and how to answer their questions.

How conversion works:
Starting February 20, PRESTO cards begin converting in daily batches over approximately 6 weeks. There are millions of cards across the Greater Toronto & Hamilton Area and Ottawa, so it simply takes time to process them all.

Overdraft Removal

Starting February 27 ‚Äî what's changing, why it's changing, and how to help frustrated customers.

üö® Key Change ‚Äì Effective Feb 27:
Registered PRESTO card taps will only be accepted if you have enough funds to pay the full local transit fare. This removes the historic ability to "go into overdraft" (negative balance) for local transit.
Why is this happening?
This change reduces fare fraud and creates a consistent experience between physical and digital PRESTO cards ‚Äî digital cards have never allowed negative balances. The rollout is tied to each card's conversion, so it activates gradually over the 6+ week conversion period.
